Mediotype's Google Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics Magento Integration

Why Is Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics Important?

Without the thorough and continuous study of your websites analytics, you would not be able to design a proper strategy for optimal marketing improvements. Without collecting the accurate data of your websites performance, you would not have the analytics to study. Google Analytics itself is obviously worth using. The data gathered and presented is a vital learning tool for any manager of online ecommerce. Using Google's Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics with Magento provides even more insights and benefits for digital merchants.

The Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics was a revamp of Google's prior ecommerce analytics which was designed to offer better insights and information into pre-purchasing shopping behavior as well as provide product performance data, actionable by a merchants marketing & technology teams. It's intention was to keep pace with the ever growing size and complexity of online retail. Data can come in many forms, all of which is useful, collecting that data and providing powerful reports is the main intent behind Google's ever advancing analytics suite.

>Offering deeper insights into the behavior of customers, Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics allows Magento retailers to make wiser decisions, and learn more about both their site and customer. The need for more specific and higher quality data sets is constantly changing and this required a more sophisticated and comprehensive analysis tool in order to understand shopper behavior and product performance. The market demand was there, and actually grew into a need; Google created the Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics feature and built it atop the Universal Analytics & ec.js foundation. This includes tracking code updates, data model changes, and new end-user reports targeted to optimize ecommerce intelligence. Together these allow for managers to better understand how customers engage with a website throughout the checkout process, and how products perform overall throughout the same process. Watch as data shows the effectiveness of on-site merchandising, which customers purchased based on an up-sell offer, and where in the sales funnel customers got lost.

Enhanced Ecommerce helps to over come many challenges that once plagued owners, mangers and marketers alike. With it you can gain deep insight into the absolute most important eCommerce KPI's; about shopper behavior, conversions, product views, product view placements, cart additions, cart abandonments, product abandonments, customer navigation, and even payment methods.

In sum, Google breaks the benefits of their Enhanced Ecommerce down into Two main sections: Insights and Optimization. Which are really the only benefits regular Google Analytics is good for as well. This powerful mechanism enables Magento merchants to optimize their customer engagement and increase results.

Magento Insights with Google's Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics

  • Within your shopping funnel, where users drop off
  • Comparing products with high views, abandonments in cart, and conversions
  • Product position across list sets, search results, up-sells, cross-sells, and related products
  • Ability to upload rich product metadata in order to refine how you analyze your data
  • Ability to create valued user segments to understand more thoroughly your customers behavior and the exact products they interact with
  • How users initiate, abandon, and complete transactions overall
  • How many products an average transaction includes
  • Average order value
  • Add-to-cart rates for purchases made after viewing product-detailed pages
  • For re-marketing campaigns, importing user segments based on ecommerce activity allows for more precise targeting
  • Measure both internal and external marketing efforts for products
  • The Order Coupon Report allows you to track revenue, transactions, unique purchases, product revenue per purchase, and average order value for order-level coupons.

Magento Optimization with Google Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics

  • Creation of product lists for onsite merchandising rules to determine which list is best at increasing customer engagement
  • Creation of product landing pages to determine which product is best at increasing customer engagement
  • Analyzing and measuring the effectiveness and impact on sales from onsite promotions
  • Overall increasing the ability to improve a sites average onsite conversion rate
  • Multiple properties within the Enhanced Ecommerce which use string values and allows managers to create any category and brand taxonomy that's relevant and desired. These insights can then drive the creation of needed landing pages and even new product categories
  • The refund support now allows Google Analytics to cover the entire shopping lifecycle for a product/customer. Having this information present helps to determine courses of action if the number of returns becomes a problem
  • The Affiliate Code reports allows managers to track revenue, transactions, and average order values that are associated with affiliate sites. Understanding which of your affiliates provides real value is important.
  • Product lists and placements can also be measured for effectiveness. You can use Product Lists to represent catalog and search results pages, related products merchandising blocks, as well as cross-sell and up-sell blocks. Product List Performance reports then allow you to see which lists appeared to which users, which products appeared in those lists, and the clicks, views, and click through rate for each list, list position, and product. Product placement, exposure, and order value matter, knowing what you want to show to who and where makes all the difference.

Functionality of Mediotype's Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics Extension for Magento

So then, how does the Google Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics Extension for Magento work? Following Magento best practices, this light weight module takes responsibility for using layout xml to remove Magentoâ€™s built in Google Analytics ga.js tracking code. The extension inserts properly formatted analytics.js with an include for the 'ec' functionality that Google requires for Enhanced Ecommerce Analytics just before the opening body tag. Ecommerce analytics calls have some key considerations, for example once a page view is sent, ecommerce data can not be sent, so we've left the page view send to be responsible for the push to analytics and hooked it just before the closing body tag.
